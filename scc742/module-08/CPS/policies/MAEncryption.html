Hexelo Medium Assurance Encryption Policy

Encryption certificates are used to protect the confidentiality and integrity of data.

Medium assurance encryption certificates are issued only after successful authentication in a face-to-face meeting using a valid government-issued photo ID, such as a driver's license, passport, or Nexus card. The Local Registration Authority (LRA) must record the details of the government ID and store these details securely before issuing the certificate.

The certificate and related private key are stored on the hard disk in the userâ€™s profile. The certificate may only be used for encryption and decryption purposes. If used for the purposes of Key Recovery and Archival certificate, it must not be archived for private key recovery. Otherwise, key archival of the private key may be permitted. Medium Assurance encryption certificates allow export of the private key or allow a one-time export of the private key.
