Hexelo High Assurance Encryption Policy

Encryption certificates are used to protect the confidentiality and integrity of data.

High assurance encryption certificates are issued only after successful authentication in a face-to-face meeting using a valid government-issued photo ID, such as a driver's license, passport, or Nexus card. The Local Registration Authority (LRA) must record the details of the government ID and store these details securely before issuing the certificate.

The certificate and related private key are stored on cryptographically secured hardware, such as a smart card, USB token, or Trusted Platform Module (TPM) chip. The certificate may only be used for encryption purposes and must not be archived for private key recovery.
